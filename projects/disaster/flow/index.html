Total aid on June 2, 2014

Up to $276.4 million

Federal and state disaster assistance can be provided through numerous agencies and channels. In the case of Disaster 4117, three federal agencies plus the State of Oklahoma are delivering relief dollars to governments, utility cooperatives, businesses, individuals and other groups, using several programs:

Federal Emergency Management Agency
U.S. Small Business Administration
U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development
State of Oklahoma

FEMA: $60.7 million

The Federal Emergency Management Agency's money stream divides three ways:

Public Assistance Grants: $41.7 million

  • 109 applicants requested assistance on 693 projects
  • 538 were awarded grants
  • 32 were approved and the award is pending
  • 93 are waiting to hear if they're eligible
  • 30 were ruled ineligible

Individuals and households program: $15.1 million

Of 15,382 who registered for this program, 3,663, or 24 percent, were found eligible.

Hazard Mitigation Grants: $3.9 million

Of the total, $409,702 went to Oklahoma Emergency Management to offset administrative costs. Twenty-one projects in 14 counties were awarded grants.

U.S. Small Business Administration: $49.8 million

The SBA approved 929 loans — 877 to homeowners and 52 to businesses — to recipients in 53 cities.

U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development: Up to $145.9 million

Oklahoma City, Moore and the State of Oklahoma received Community Block Development Grants in two rounds:

First Round
  • State: $10.6 million
  • Moore: $26.3 million
Second Round
  • Oklahoma City: up to $83.1 million for disasters 2011-2013
  • Moore: $25.9 million

State of Oklahoma: $20 million

Oklahoma Emergency Management returned $25 million to the state's rainy day fund, leaving $20 million available for disaster assistance. Of that:

  • $8.2 million was used to pay the state's share of FEMA's public and individual assistance
  • $11.8 million will be spent on other disaster relief projects
